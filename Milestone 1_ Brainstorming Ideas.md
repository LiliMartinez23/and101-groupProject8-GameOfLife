## Conway's Game of Life
**Mobile**
- How uniquely mobile is the product experience?
    - When using touch gestures such as pinch, drag, tap or toggle cells, this could be a mildly unique mobile app.
- What makes your app more than a glorified website?
    - Offline simulation, and possibly share the patterns as images.

**Story**
- How compelling is the story around this app once completed?
    - A sandbox to explore the behavior and patterns in a famous mathematical simulation.
- How clear is the value of this app to your audience?
    - The app is more tailored towards computer science and math nerds than a general audience, but not limited to.
- How well would your friends or peers respond to this product idea?
    - The response would be along the lines of a fun game rather than a life changing tool.

**Market**
- How large or unique is the market for this app?
    - People who are into math, simulations, and/or art. This app is unique but not huge.
- What's the size and scale of your potential user base?
    - This app is not to be expanded into a mass-market productivity app. Driven towards students, people with hobbies, and to encourage curiosity.
- Does this app provide huge value to a niche group of people?
    - Yes, this app is valuable to a small niche group of people (math and CS enthusiasts).
- Do you have a well-defined audience of people for this app?
    - Yes, students learning cellular automata, programmers, math enthusiasts, etc. 

**Habit**
- How habit-forming or addictive is this app?
    - This is not really a habit-forming or addictive app. This is more like a fun game.
- How frequently would an average user open and use this app?
    - The avereage user would probably open this app before a class, quick entertainment, or to show a friend.
- Does an average user just consume your app, or do they use it to create something?
    - Users will be creating patterns and experiment with the rules, saving various and interesting states.

**Scope**
- How well-formed is the scope for this app?
    - The scope is very clear. There's a grid, rules, controls, pattern saving/sharing.
- How technically challenging will it be to complete this app by the deadline?
    - The core logic is simple, the challenge is performance and smooth animations.
- Is a stripped-down version of this app still interesting to build?
    - Yes, basic rules, a small grid, start/stop/reset.
    - An advanced feature would be sharing the patterns.
- How clearly defined is the product you want to build?
    - It's a known simulation with well-defined machanics.

## Pomodoro App
**Mobile**
- How uniquely mobile is the product experience?
    - The experience for a Pomodoro timer is similar across desktop and mobile.
- What makes your app more than a glorified website?
    - Notifications, lock-screen widget, focus modes, etc.

**Story**
- How compelling is the story around this app once completed?
    - A productivity app that actually matches how I work.
- How clear is the value of this app to your audience?
    - It's a clear as it gets. The app help's people focus, avoid distractions, and structure their work/tasks.
- How well would your friends or peers respond to this product idea?
    - Unfortunately, my friends are not the best at time management and tend to procrastinate a lot. This app would be of great interest for them.

**Market**
- How large or unique is the market for this app?
    - The market is huge, but also crowded. This idea isn't unique.
- What's the size and scale of your potential user base?
    - Anyone needing help focusing, avoid distractions, and structure their work will be the potential user base.
- Does this app provide huge value to a niche group of people?
    - It can, if we specialize it to certain users. For example, users with ADHD.
- Do you have a well-defined audience of people for this app?
    - Students in general.

**Habit**
- How habit-forming or addictive is this app?
    - This is indeed habit-forming, but maybe not addictive.
- How frequently would an average user open and use this app?
    - Ideally, multiple times a day during work or study sessions.
- Does an average user just consume your app, or do they use it to create something?
    - They create time blocks and focus sessions when using the app.

**Scope**
- How well-formed is the scope for this app?
    - The scope is clear: timer, sessions/breaks, basic stats, and notifications.
- How technically challenging will it be to complete this app by the deadline?
    - Technically, the app doesn't contain anything super challenging, a lot of the features are straightforward.
- Is a stripped-down version of this app still interesting to build?
    - Yes, this app will be a simpler version of a Pomodoro timer.
- How clearly defined is the product you want to build?
    - It's a focused timer with scheduled breaks and simple progress tracking.

## Eco-friendly App
**Mobile**
- How uniquely mobile is the product experience?
    - Leverage mobile features like location and camera usage. Notifications for eco-friendly tips and reminders.
- What makes your app more than a glorified website?
    - Access to GPS, camera, notifications, and offline. Integrate with your health app to track steps, walking, and biking.

**Story**
- How compelling is the story around this app once completed?
    - This app helps people reduce their environmental footprint through small, trackable actions.
- How clear is the value of this app to your audience?
    - By helping people develop healthy eco-friendly habits this encourages them to continue making a greater impact for a cleaner environment.
- How well would your friends or peers respond to this product idea?
    - Environmentalists will likely be interested in this app as well as friends who are willing to develope positive habits.

**Market**
- How large or unique is the market for this app?
    - This market is broad with eco-consious communities, students, and families. Less like a productivity app which gives it that uniqueness.
- What's the size and scale of your potential user base?
    - Quite large. Millions of people care about maintaining the environment fundamentally. Adopting these habits depends on how simple and rewarding the experience is.
- Does this app provide huge value to a niche group of people?
    - It could provide a big value to motivate and inspire users like zero-waste enthusiasts, sustainability clubs, etc.
- Do you have a well-defined audience of people for this app?
    - Young adults trying to build sustainable habits.

**Habit**
- How habit-forming or addictive is this app?
    - Very habit-forming, since the app centers around forming and performing eco-friendly habits.
- How frequently would an average user open and use this app?
    - Ideally daily or a few times a week.
- Does an average user just consume your app, or do they use it to create something?
    - They use this app to create or develope eco-friendly habits to contribute to and to maintain the environment.

**Scope**
- How well-formed is the scope for this app?
    - By focusing on a small set of core features such as habit list, logging, and a progress board.
- How technically challenging will it be to complete this app by the deadline?
    - The app is a basic habit tracker with simple metrics.
- Is a stripped-down version of this app still interesting to build?
    - Yes, a simple eco-habit tracker is still very meaningfull and portfolio worthy.
- How clearly defined is the product you want to build?
    - Without clear core features and simple stats, the app takes a risk at becoming a vague sustainability app.
